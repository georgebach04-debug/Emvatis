I.

An island of few words

Kythnos is one of the least-visited islands in the Cyclades — barely ninety minutes from Athens by ferry, yet a world away from the crowds of its famous neighbours. There's no performance here, no rush to impress: just whitewashed villages, dry gold hills, and a hospitality that doesn't need to try.

It's an island built for slowness. Once you arrive, the only real decision left is which beach.

II.

More coastline than you can use

With over a hundred beaches scattered along its shores, Kythnos has a different shoreline for every mood — long sandy stretches near Merichas for lazy mornings, hidden coves reachable only by dirt track or boat, and Kolona, the island's famous double sandbar, where the sea meets you from both sides at once.

Most days, the hardest choice is whether to stay where you are.

III.

Evenings that take their time

As the heat lifts, Merichas slips into its evening rhythm — fishing boats easing into the harbour, tables filling along the water, the first lights coming on in the hills. Further inland, Dryopida's tiled rooftops and Chora's narrow lanes hold a different pace entirely, and on the far coast, the thermal springs at Loutra have been drawing travellers since antiquity.

Nothing here is in a hurry. That's rather the point.

Where the road forks

From the door, the island opens up

Merichas sits at the centre of it all — close enough to walk to the water, near enough to everything else to make the rest of Kythnos feel within easy reach.

Martinakia Beach

A short stroll from Emvatis. Calm, shallow water and soft sand — the easy, everyday option.

Merichas Port

Ferries, tavernas, and the first lights of the evening. The heartbeat of this side of the island.

Kolona Beach

The island's famous sandbar, sea on both sides. A scenic drive or a short boat ride — always worth it.

Loutra

Natural hot springs on the far coast, known since antiquity. A whole different afternoon, across the island.

Dryopida & Chora

Tiled roofs, whitewashed lanes, a folklore museum and quiet squares. A short drive inland, a different century.
